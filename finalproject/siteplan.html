Maison Kemet

Paris × Cairo

Site Name & Vision

Name: Maison Kemet

Reasoning: The name creates an immediate, highly chic juxtaposition. Maison evokes the established craftsmanship of a Parisian haute couture house, while Kemet (the indigenous name for ancient Egypt) references the opulent history and striking silhouettes of pharaonic royalty. It establishes a brand identity that is both architecturally modern and historically rich.

Domain: maisonkemet.com

Purpose & Scope

Maison Kemet serves as a digital couture showroom. The primary purpose is to provide an elite, minimalist interface for prospective clients to explore custom gown textiles and silhouettes. The site will utilize JavaScript to dynamically fetch and display JSON data comparing luxury fabrics (such as brocade, silk organza, and lamé), and will include an interactive form for clients to submit initial measurements and commission inquiries.

User Scenarios

To ensure usability and targeted functionality, the site will address the following client inquiries:

  • 01. "How does the structural integrity of metallic lamé compare to traditional silk organza for an evening gala dress?"
    (Addressed via the dynamically generated JSON fabric database).
  • 02. "Where can I formally submit my measurements and event details to initiate a bespoke design consultation?"
    (Addressed via the clearly navigable HTML form action page).

Editorial Palette

The color scheme strictly adheres to a minimalist, high-fashion aesthetic. Whitespace is heavily utilized as a design principle, while stark black and subtle greys provide structure. Gold is reserved strictly for interaction and accentuation.

Alabaster#FAF9F6 • Base
Obsidian#111111 • Text/Lines
Montaigne Grey#595A5E • Borders
Luxor Gold#8B6D28 • Accents

Typography

The typographic layout relies on high contrast between classical elegance and modern geometry, a signature of luxury editorial design.

  • Bodoni Moda (Serif): Used exclusively for `h1`, `h2`, and `h3` tags. Its dramatic thick-and-thin strokes convey immediate luxury and editorial authority.
  • Jost (Sans-Serif): Used for all body copy, navigation, and list items. Inspired by Futura, its stark, geometric simplicity provides highly accessible, modern readability that grounds the ornate headers.
